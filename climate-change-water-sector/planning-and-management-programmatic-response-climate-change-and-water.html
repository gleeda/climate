Planning and Management: Programmatic Response to Climate Change and Water

Below are descriptions of documents developed by the EPA National Water Program, detailing programmatic efforts to address climate change impacts to water resources. To download the documents, scroll to the bottom of the page. 
 
2016 Highlights of Progress Report Released

2016 Workplan: EPA National Water Program Response to Climate Change
The Workplan lays out the actions that the EPA National Water Program plans to take in 2016 to implement the “EPA National Water Program 2012 Strategy: Response to Climate Change." It is intended to be a roadmap to guide program planning and inform decision-makers during the Agency’s planning and budgeting process. The 2016 Workplan builds on work conducted in 2012-2014. View and download the document.
 

2015 Workplan: EPA National Water Program Response to Climate Change 
The Workplan lays out the actions that the EPA National Water Program planned to take in 2015. 
View and download the document.


 

EPA Office of Water Climate Change Adaptation Implementation Plan (2014)
The Implementation Plan provides an overview of the opportunities available to the Office of Water within the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency to respond climate challenges.  The Plan addresses specific challenges for the operation of national programs to protect the water quality and drinking water in a changing climate. 
View and download the document.

EPA National Water Program 2012 Strategy: Response to Climate Change 
The Strategy sets out EPA's long-term goals and specific actions to prepare for and build resilience to the impacts of a changing climate on water resources. The Strategy is organized around five programmatic vision areas: protecting water infrastructure; coastal and ocean waters; watersheds; water quality, and working with Tribes. View and download the document.
 
To order copies of the EPA National Water Program 2012 Strategy: Response to Climate Change, visit: http://www.epa.gov/nscep/ordering. Ordering can be done online, by phone, or by mail. The document number is: 850-K-12-004.
