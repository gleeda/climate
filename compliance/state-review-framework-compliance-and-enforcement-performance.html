Frequent Questions

  • Who uses the State Review Framework?

    EPA and states use the framework to assess their performance in enforcing water, air and waste permits, and as oversight of state and EPA enforcement.

  • How are the reviews conducted?

    EPA conducts regional and state reviews on a five year cycle, using:

    • national and state data,
    • enforcement file reviews,
    • commitments made in annual agreements and
    • discussions with senior management.

    Once the review is completed, EPA creates a report. If the report identifies issues for resolution, EPA and the state will address them collaboratively.  EPA will capture the resulting measures in future grant agreements.

  • Who does the review?

    States are authorized by EPA to implement the statutory requirements of the CWA, CAA, and RCRA. States that are authorized to implement a program will be reviewed by the EPA Regions under the SRF. For states that are not authorized and EPA still retains the authority (called Direct Implementation programs), EPA headquarters conducts the SRF review of the Region’s implementation of the enforcement program.

  • Where can I find data used in these reports?

    The data is available in several forms on EPA's Enforcement and Compliance History Online's (ECHO) SRF page.

  • What is the current status of reviews?

    EPA completed reviews:

    • Round 1 from FY 2004-07
    • Round 2 from FY 2008-12

    Round 3 began in FY 2013 and will conclude in FY 2017.

  • How else is EPA strengthening the oversight of states?

    On December 13, 2013, EPA released the National Strategy for Improving Oversight of State Enforcement Performance.  The three main elements of this strategy are:

    1. The Escalation Approach to Problem-Solving:  A series of escalating steps intended to provide consistent guidance to the regions in their review of and response to state enforcement performance issues.
    2. Plans for Addressing Significant Issues:  EPA Regions and states should work together to address identified significant individual state performance issues.
    3. Transparency Efforts:  Efforts intended to provide the public with timely, high quality information on state and federal enforcement performance can motivate government to improve.
  • What are the national performance expectations for states?

    The SRF reviews evaluate performance under the CAA stationary sources requirements, the CWA National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System requirements, and the RCRA Subtitle C programs against benchmarks established in existing program policies and guidance. The relevant policies and guidance are listed in the State Oversight Resources and Guidance Documents section.

The State Review Framework (SRF) consistently assesses EPA and state enforcement of the Clean Water Act (CWA), the Clean Air Act (CAA), and Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA). Designed collaboratively in 2004 by EPA and the Environmental Council of the States (ECOS), Exit EPA works in partnership with each state to create a final SRF report. These SRF reports allow EPA to identify recommendations for improvement to ensure fair and consistent enforcement and compliance programs across the states.
